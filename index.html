Recovery Connect for Napier Bailiffs

Recovery Connect Resource for Napier Bailiffs
This webpage serves as an instructional Recovery Connect resource. Below are videos and frequently asked questions covering what you as a bailiff will be doing on a regular basis within the software. If you have further questions or concerns, please contact the office and they will work to address them.
What is Recovery Connect?
RecoveryConnect is a comprehensive asset recovery software platform that provides full case management. RecoveryConnect seamlessly integrates recovery case management, data-driven automation, and compliance solutions into a single platform. It is a cloud based solution that will be replacing DASH as it is discontinued.
Frequently Asked Questions
  • Is Recovery Connect secure and compliant with industry standards? - Yes, Recovery Connect adheres to strict security protocols and is compliant with relevant industry standards. It ensures the confidentiality and integrity of sensitive data, making it a secure choice for asset recovery operations.
  • Can I access Recovery Connect from any device? - Being a cloud-based platform, Recovery Connect is accessible from any device with internet connectivity. This includes smartphones, tablets, and computers, providing flexibility and convenience for users on the go and is also accompanied by the RCM mobile app.
  • What kind of support is available for Recovery Connect users? - Support for Recovery Connect includes a dedicated helpdesk, user manuals, online tutorials, and a technical support team. For any issues or queries, users can reach out to the office, consult this webpage, or read the manual.
  • Account - in Recovery Connect, your bailiff account is treated as its own company/independent contractor. This means that you need to accept or decline new cases, addresses, holds/closes, and more. It also means that once you add your data to a file such as photos, you need to transfer them to Napier Bailiffs as they are treated as a separate company. If you do not, the office will not be able to view them or proceed with client communications.
  • Where can I find my files/cases? - You can find your cases via the 3 buttons at the very top of the screen, hovering over the cases dropdown in the blue ribbon at the top of the screen, or by searching for a piece of related data within the search bar in the blue ribbon and clicking on the desired file/case. In the Mobile app, you can find them by searching for them, or by checking your to-do list.
  • Where can I find the case documents/attachments? - You can find your cases documents by first finding the case, then looking for the attachments tab on the case. To view or download an attachment, just click on the little gear icon next to the desired attachment and either hit "view" or "download".
  • How do I create a case update in recovery connect? - You can create case updates either in the mobile app or web app by first finding the case. Once in the file, you can click on the updates section, then find the "+" button and create your update. After the update is written, save it as a public update.
  • How do I upload non recovery related photos? - You can upload photos not related to the recovery by first finding the case, then selecting the attachments/photos tab on it. Once there your can upload the photos by clicking on the "+" symbol and adding them. Then click on the gear icon next to them and follow the prompts to share the photos with the client/Napier Bailiffs.
  • How do I complete a recovery in Recovery Connect? - You can complete a recovery in Recovery Connect either using the web application or the dedicated mobile application. In both, you will first need to find the file, then "on-hook" the collateral. Once "on-hooked", the file will be ready to have a recovery completed, on which you can access on the file itself in the web app, or on your to-do list in the mobile app. From there, follow all of the prompts involved in the recover process until you see it removed from your to-do list. The office will then acknowledge your recovery.
